Explore Ghana Coastals and Forts

Ghana’s forts and castles were built by European powers between the 15th and 19th centuries along the coast, mainly for trade. The Portuguese were the first, constructing Elmina Castle in 1482. Other nations like the Dutch, British, and Danes followed, each building forts for gold and later the transatlantic slave trade. These structures became key points for holding enslaved Africans before shipping them abroad. Cape Coast Castle and Elmina Castle are among the most well-known. After independence in 1957, Ghana preserved many as historical monuments. Today, they stand as symbols of colonialism, slavery, and resilience.
